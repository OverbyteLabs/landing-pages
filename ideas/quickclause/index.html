The subscription shall renew automatically for successive one-year terms unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the end of the then-current term.

Provider's total aggregate liability shall not exceed the fees paid in the prior 30-day period. Customer shall indemnify and hold harmless Provider from any third-party claim arising from Customer's use of the Service.

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of England and Wales, exclusive jurisdiction of the courts therein.

Critical

Auto-renewal: 90-day notice window

Industry standard is 30 days. 90 days is unusually long — easy to miss the cancellation window.

Critical

Broad third-party indemnity

Indemnity for "any" third-party claim is uncommonly wide. Consider negotiating a cap or exclusions.

Review

Liability cap: 30-day fees only

Very low cap for a multi-year commitment. Standard is 12 months of fees paid.

Trained on thousands of commercial contracts. Three severity levels — so you know what needs a lawyer, and what you can accept.

Critical

Auto-renewal traps

Long notice windows and evergreen clauses that lock you in for another year without warning.

Critical

Broad indemnity clauses

Open-ended indemnity obligations that extend to third-party claims without caps or carve-outs.

Critical

IP ownership sweeps

Clauses that assign IP created during the engagement to the other party, including pre-existing work.

Review

Low liability caps

Caps below 3 months of fees are well below the commercial norm. Flag for renegotiation.

Unilateral change rights
